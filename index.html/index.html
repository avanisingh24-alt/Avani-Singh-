Wire Department Process Improvement — DMAIC Initiative

Designed and led a full Lean Six Sigma DMAIC proposal targeting systemic inefficiencies in a Commercial Banking wire operations division. The wire department was processing ~310 daily transactions with a 24-minute average cycle time — three times the industry benchmark — and a 3.2% error rate generating over $340K in annual rework costs. Conducted fishbone analysis, 5-Why root cause investigation, Pareto defect analysis, and a full SIPOC mapping exercise to identify and prioritize solutions.

  • Identified top 3 root causes accounting for 77% of all defects: data entry errors (38%), missing documentation (22%), and compliance holds (17%)
  • Designed 6 prioritized solutions including intelligent input automation, risk-tiered dual-control routing, and a digital SOP portal
  • Built comprehensive Control Plan with real-time dashboard KPIs, daily stand-up governance, and response plans for each CTQ metric
  • Projected process sigma improvement from 1.52σ → 3.2σ with a 4-month payback period
